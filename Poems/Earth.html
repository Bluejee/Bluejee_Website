Earth

The Earth once a lonely place,
Gave man a chance to live,
Man was happy and filled with grace,
And to earth, he used to give.

As time progressed,
And Man grew old,
He gained power,
And became too bold.

Earth being gentle and graceful,
Was happy for man as one can be,
Gave man everything his handful,
But man for power was too thirsty.

Man hurt her feelings,
And hurt her pride,
And made her watch,
As the animals cried.

Earth now had seen enough,
To teach man his rightful place,
This time it was not a bluff,
But truly her angry face.

With a burst of wrath,
she growled at man,
Enough to scare him,
for his lifespan.

And with that, she reminded him that,
She was the master, not the slave.
